Places to explore

At over 214m high the cliffs of Moher are a spectacular site and a must visit experience for anyone visiting Co Clare or travelling the Wild Atlantic May. The cliffs are located between Lahinch and Doolin ( both also worth a visit) and boasts a modern and interesting Interpretive center complete with shops and restaurants. For the very active there is a 12km cliff walk bringing you down to hags head to the South. From nearby Doolin you can get a ferry to the Aran Islands or see the cliffs from the sea. Ireland's second most visited tourist attraction never disappoints.
For more information please visit Cliffs of Moher.

Over the years Lahinch has established itself as one of Irelands foremost surfing destinations. As well as impressive and consistent waves Lahinch now boasts a host of surfing clubs and schools to cater for all ages and capabilites. In addition to the surfing, Lahinch is a major tourist resort with pubs, restaurants and hotels/ hostels close by. Lahinch is a blue flag beach on the Wild Atlantic Way and is a short drive from the cliffs of Moher and Doolin.
For more information check out Lahinch Information.

The name Burren is derived from an Irish word Boireann meaning "a rocky place" The stone that makes up the Burren was laid down during the Carboniforous period and the rock was most likely exposed by over farming by early settlers combined with a period of intense rainfall. The Burren has a host of activities to offer the visitor with many well marked walking trails, educational tours, historical sites such as Pol na Bron and attractions such as Ailwee caves.
For more information please visit Burren National Park.
